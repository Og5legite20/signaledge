Trading Glossary

Plain-English definitions of the key terms used across SignalEdge.

New to trading? This glossary explains, in clear language, the technical terms and concepts you will encounter throughout the SignalEdge platform and our Education section. Terms are listed alphabetically.

Accumulation

The first phase of a market cycle, occurring after a decline, in which patient investors quietly build positions while sentiment is still pessimistic and prices move sideways in a range.

Bear Market

A sustained period of falling prices, defined by a pattern of lower highs and lower lows and typically accompanied by widespread fear and pessimism.

Breakout

A move in which price pushes decisively through a level of support or resistance. Breakouts backed by high volume are more likely to hold than those on weak volume.

Bull Market

A sustained period of rising prices, defined by higher highs and higher lows and usually accompanied by optimism and growing participation.

Candlestick

A charting element that shows the open, high, low, and close of an asset over a single period. The body spans the open-to-close range and the wicks mark the extremes.

Confidence Score

On SignalEdge, a percentage that reflects how strongly the underlying indicators agree with one another. It measures technical alignment, not the probability of profit.

Death Cross

A bearish chart pattern in which a shorter-term moving average crosses below a longer-term one, suggesting that downward momentum is building.

Divergence

A condition where price and an indicator (such as RSI or volume) move in opposite directions, often warning that the current trend is quietly weakening.

Doji

A candlestick whose open and close are almost equal, signalling indecision between buyers and sellers and sometimes preceding a reversal.

Drawdown

The peak-to-trough decline in the value of an account or asset, usually expressed as a percentage. Managing drawdown is central to long-term survival as a trader.

Engulfing Pattern

A two-candle pattern in which the second candle’s body completely covers the first, signalling a potential shift in momentum in the direction of the second candle.

FOMO

“Fear of missing out”: the emotional urge to buy an asset that is rising rapidly. FOMO typically peaks near market tops and leads to poorly-timed entries.

Forex

The foreign-exchange market, where national currencies are traded in pairs such as EUR/USD, GBP/USD, or USD/NGN.

Golden Cross

A bullish chart pattern in which a shorter-term moving average crosses above a longer-term one, suggesting that upward momentum is strengthening.

Halving

A pre-programmed event, occurring roughly every four years, in which the reward for mining new Bitcoin is cut in half, reducing the rate at which new supply enters the market.

Hammer

A candlestick with a small body and a long lower wick, indicating that sellers pushed price down but buyers regained control — often a bullish sign after a downtrend.

Liquidity

A measure of how easily an asset can be bought or sold without significantly affecting its price. Highly liquid markets make technical analysis more reliable.

Market Capitalisation

The total value of an asset’s circulating supply, calculated by multiplying its current price by the number of units in circulation.

Momentum

The speed and strength of a price movement. Momentum indicators such as RSI attempt to measure whether a move is accelerating or fading.

Moving Average (MA)

An indicator that smooths price data by averaging it over a set number of periods, revealing the underlying trend. SignalEdge uses 7-day and 14-day moving averages.

Overbought

A condition, often indicated by an RSI reading above 70, in which an asset may have risen too far too fast and could be due for a pullback.

Oversold

A condition, often indicated by an RSI reading below 30, in which an asset may have fallen too far too fast and could be due for a bounce.

Position Sizing

The process of deciding how much capital to commit to a trade, based on your account size and the distance to your stop loss. A cornerstone of risk management.

Relative Strength Index (RSI)

A momentum oscillator, measured on a scale of 0 to 100, that gauges whether an asset is overbought or oversold based on the speed of recent price changes.

Resistance

A price level where rising prices tend to stall and reverse, because selling interest becomes strong enough to overcome buying pressure.

Stop Loss

A pre-set order that closes a trade automatically once price reaches a level at which the original idea is considered wrong, capping the loss on that trade.

Support

A price level where falling prices tend to stop and reverse, because buying interest becomes strong enough to overcome selling pressure.

Volatility

A measure of how much and how quickly a price moves. Crypto and forex markets are known for high volatility, which creates both opportunity and risk.

Volume

The number of units of an asset traded in a given period. Volume confirms the strength — or reveals the weakness — behind a price move.
